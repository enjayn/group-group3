LOK CHING is one of the traditional foods available in Kelantan. Its name is quite unique as it is not a term or pronunciation in the Kelantan dialect but is believed to originate from the Thai language. The main ingredient to make this lok ching is fish filling. The name Lok ching is taken from the Thai word "บอล เบิ ร์ น" which means "BEBOLA BAKAR".

The basic ingredient of Lok ching is a fish/meat/chicken/crab/shrimp filling that is ground and mixed with starch, salt and shaped into a round shape like a BALL, or OTHER UNIQUE SHAPE. Raw lok cing will be pricked on a skewer and BURNT over embers. Once cooked, it will be dipped into a SPECIAL THAI SOS

The lok ching business revolution is growing with the passage of time and now lok ching has its own "fans". This unique food from Thailand is starting to be sold on the Malaysian - Thai border such as in Kelantan, Perlis and Kedah and the response is getting warmer

Renewal after renewal is made from the point of view of traditional cooking methods (using charcoal) in improved by the presence of a lok ching burner using a gas / electric stove. Apart from that, the emergence of lok ching varieties from various sources (chicken/meat/fish/squid/shrimp) in various forms adds to the uniqueness of this fast food.
